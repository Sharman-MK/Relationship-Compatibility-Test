What is Coercive Control?

Coercive control is a pattern of acts of threat, humiliation, intimidation, or abuse used by one person to dominate their partner. It includes monitoring your phone, telling you what to wear, isolating you from friends, controlling your money, and limiting your choices.

Forced Marriage vs. Arranged Marriage

An **arranged marriage** is where families help introduce partners, but both individuals have the complete, unpressured right to say **no** at any stage. A **forced marriage** is a union where one or both people do not consent or feel pressured, guilted, threatened, or emotionally blackmailed into agreeing. Forced marriage is a violation of human rights and a serious form of relationship abuse.

The Cycle of Apologies ("Love-Bombing")

Controlling partners often alternate between periods of extreme jealousy or anger and intense affection, gift-giving, and apologies (love-bombing). They may say "I only check your phone because I love you so much" or "I want to marry you right away because you're my soulmate." Recognize that these statements are often used to override your doubts and maintain control.
